Religious Studies & Philosophy Teacher
Full-time Opening

Northfield Mount Hermon School, an independent co-educational boarding school in western Massachusetts, seeks a dynamic individual to teach religious studies and philosophy in a collaborative humanities curriculum designed to engage students in exploring the ways people are shaped by religions and philosophies. This position begins with the fall semester of the 2026-2027 academic year.

The successful applicant will have at least a bachelor’s degree in religious studies, philosophy, or a related field (master’s preferred) and the ability to teach a variety of introductory and intermediate level religious studies and philosophy courses, including introductory religious studies and philosophy, world religions, ethics, and philosophy.  Additional qualifications include strong interpersonal and communication skills, demonstrated ability to work collaboratively with colleagues within an interdisciplinary curriculum, and knowledge of current technologies for teaching.  

Candidates must be familiar with boarding school life and have an interest in making a positive impact on the lives of adolescents. They will demonstrate experience in equity and inclusion work that promotes a diverse community environment and a clear understanding of anti-bias education.

Responsibilities of the position include a combination of classroom teaching, student advising, resident dormitory supervision, and coaching or equivalents. Candidates are encouraged to include in their application experiences that may support our athletic and student life program.

We reasonably expect to compensate for this position between $48,163 and $57,049, based on years of experience.  Final compensation may vary depending on workload and relevant experience.

Description:
Athletic Director

Salary and Benefits
Salary range is $115,000 to $130,000 commensurate with experience. NMH offers a generous benefits package as well, including tuition remission and on-campus housing.

The Opportunity
Northfield Mount Hermon School seeks a dynamic, student-centered Athletic
Director to lead a highly competitive interscholastic athletics program
encompassing 67 interscholastic and 19 varsity teams. The Athletic Director
will serve on the school’s leadership team and report to the Head of School
and the Associate Head of School.
Grounded in a strong commitment to student health, wellness, and balance,
the Athletic Director will provide strategic vision and day-to-day leadership
for all aspects of athletics. The director will lead a program that promotes
physical well-being and competitive integrity while aligning fully with the
school’s educational mission, values, and commitment to developing citizens
and scholars.
The Athletic Director is a visible campus leader who collaborates closely with
faculty colleagues, academic departments, residential life, health and
wellness, admissions, advancement, and parent communities. This role is
ideal for an experienced educator-coach-administrator who understands the
rhythms of boarding school life and is energized by mentoring adolescents.

Description:
Teaching Fellowship Program

 

The NMH Fellowship Program is a two-year, immersive professional pathway designed for emerging educators with little to no prior teaching experience who seek to develop as reflective practitioners while completing a master’s degree.

Rooted in Northfield Mount Hermon’s mission to educate the head, heart, and hand, the program combines intentional mentorship, progressive teaching responsibility, and graduate study to prepare fellows for long-term careers in education and independent school leadership.

For the 2026-2027 academic year we will be hiring a Program Fellow for the Office of Multicultural Affairs.
We already hired our English and History Teaching Fellows for the 2026-2027 academic year.

I have a question about a faculty position or the faculty application process. Whom do I contact?

Email faculty_recruitment@nmhschool.org with faculty-specific questions.

I have a question about a staff position or the staff application process. Whom do I contact?

Email hr@nmhschool.org with staff-specific questions.

Does time off roll over at the end of the fiscal year? 

Yes: 50% of sick personal time rolls over, up to five working days. At least 112.5 hours of your vacation time can roll over each fiscal year; the amount increases up to 240 hours based on employee designation and years of service.

When does my health insurance coverage begin?

Your health insurance coverage begins the first of the month after you start. For example, if you start work on Feb. 18, coverage will begin on March 1. 

What can I pay for with my Health Savings Account?

You can use your Health Savings Account (HSA) money for a wide range of IRS-qualified medical expenses, including deductibles, copayments, prescriptions, over-the-counter medicines, dental care, vision care, medical devices, and even travel for medical care. See the complete list. 

Can I use my Flexible Spending Account to pay for dependent care?

Yes. Please note that on Jan. 1, 2026, the Dependent Care Flexible Spending Account (DCFSA) limit will increase to $7,500 for single individuals or married couples filing jointly and $3,750 for married couples filing separately.
