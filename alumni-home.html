Alumni Stories

NMH fosters lifelong friendships, a love of learning, and living with humanity and purpose. Our alumni take a wide range of paths in life. Some are prominent, visible figures, and others contribute meaningfully to society in equally important though less public ways. 

Explore stories of fellow alums and the ways they carry NMH with them.

Carolyn Kuan ‘95

Carolyn Kuan ’95, internationally recognized conductor and Music Director of the Hartford Symphony Orchestra, joins her former NMH teacher and mentor, Sheila Heffernon, on the Head, Heart, and Hand podcast. Together, they reflect on the beginnings of their student–teacher relationship, their shared journeys into conducting, and the role of music in building community and connection.

Read More about Carolyn Kuan ‘95

Samuel Calagione ’88

Sam Calagione ’88, founder of Dogfish Head and a pioneer in craft brewing and distilling, shares his NMH journey, how he found his passion, and how the school’s “head, heart, and hand” mission shapes his company and philanthropy.

Read More about Samuel Calagione ’88

Caitrin Rogers ’00

Caitrin Rogers ’00, Academy Award–winning documentary producer, reflects on the confidence she gained at NMH, the art of storytelling, and her work on acclaimed films including 20 Feet from Stardom and Miss Americana.

Read More about Caitrin Rogers ’00

Valerie Jarrett ‘74

Valerie Jarrett ’74 is CEO of the Barack Obama Foundation and a senior distinguished fellow at the University of Chicago Law School. She served as senior advisor to President Obama from 2009 to 2017 and is the author of the bestselling memoir "Finding My Voice".

Read More about Valerie Jarrett ‘74

Mariah Calagione ‘89

Mariah Calagione ’89 is the co-founder of Dogfish Head, the pioneering craft brewery she launched with her husband, Sam Calagione ’88. Over nearly three decades, they’ve grown the brand to include a distillery, restaurants, and an inn, before merging with Boston Beer Co. Mariah also devoted more than a decade to NMH as a trustee, including six years as board chair.

Read More about Mariah Calagione ‘89

Toni Bush ‘74

Toni Bush is the retired global head of government affairs for News Corp, whose holdings include the Wall Street Journal, HarperCollins, and Realtor.com. She played a role in key issues as varied as vertical integration, in which the same company owns both content and its distribution; funding for public television; and spectrum sharing.
