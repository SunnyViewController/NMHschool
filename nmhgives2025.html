25-26 Main Stage Theater Talk-Back

1:30 to 2:30 p.m. EST
We have had a busy and exciting season of shows on the NMH stage this year! There has been a mix of student and faculty directed productions that have been both fun and meaningful experiences for the community on campus. Thursday morning and afternoon, we are holding two Zoom calls for folks to hear about what we've been working on, and ask questions about the activity happening behind the scenes that made this year of shows possible. If you want to learn more about theater at NMH, get an inside scoop from the students and faculty involved, and maybe get a look into the future of the program please join us for either or both of these Zoom events! We look forward to meeting with you virtually this week! 

Class of 1967: ‘67 - Cooler Than Ever!

2:00 to 3:00 p.m. EST
Not that we’re surprised, but “6-7” is all the rage. We are, like, totally cool! (Just ask anyone under 15 if you need an explanation.) Not only that, but ’67 is definitely a PRIME group of classmates – nothing can divide us! Let’s reconnect on Zoom for a casual conversation about what makes ’67 so special. Check out our 50th reunion class pin for some hints.

We can't wait to see you and catch up!  Call will be facilitated by Chris Anderson, Wil Everhart and Mary-Jane Atwater

Class of 1959 Gathering

4:00 to 5:00 p.m. EST

We’d especially enjoy welcoming those who haven’t participated in the past. Just think: no traveling, no fees, and we still get to see each other! So grab a snack, find a comfortable seat, and let’s go Zooming. (Please note: This will be a politics-free zone.)

Looking Back and Moving Forward: NMH Dance Meetup

Class of 1969: Back to the Future

5:00 to 6:00 p.m. EST
Remember the end of the movie “Back to the Future”? Doc Emmett Brown flies back to the present to tell Marty McFly and his girlfriend Jennifer that they have to go into the future with him because there is a problem with their kids. Let’s have some fun using this as a theme on our gathering: If we had an opportunity to share some of our late ’60s NMH experiences with current and future NMH students, what would we tell them? How can we journey from our past to inform their future? We are delighted that NMH archivist (and honorary member of our class) Peter Weis ’78 will join us to hear about our special memories and insights from our time on campus and tell us how that experience has changed in the present. For example, we may remember:

  • Writing letters and having to wait at least a day for a reply — no texting!
  • Eating a set meal instead of grazing at a buffet — no vegan options!
  • Strict dress codes — no sweats to class!
  • Mandatory chapel
  • Etc.

NMH Outdoor Programs Meet Up

The offerings may have changed over the years, but we’re still focused on fostering a love and appreciation for all things outdoors. Please join us!

Inside the Huddle with Current Women Coaches and Alumnae

6:00 to 7:30 p.m. EST
Want to learn more about girls’ athletics at NMH? Join current coaches and students and former NMH athletes as we talk about the girls’ athletics program and our experience in athletics. This conversation will celebrate National Girls and Women in Sports Day, marked earlier this month. Come join us to hear about the past, present, and future of girls’ athletics at NMH!

Featuring NMH coaches Amy Haefele (soccer), Maria Echezarreta ’20 (soccer), Grace Heeps'19 (basketball), Kelsey Roberts (field hockey), Katie Clark (lacrosse), and Bri Wood (hockey).

 

Veterans Gathering

7:00 to 8:00 p.m. EST
Please join Tom Baxter ’59, Captain, U.S. Army Corps of Engineers, and Noah Babala-Money '20, U.S. Navy Emergency Room Nurse, along with fellow NMH veterans for the fourth annual gathering of NMH veterans. Reconnect with old friends and meet new ones who share your experience of serving our country after graduating from NMH. Noah currently serves at Naval Medical Center San Diego, the Navy’s highest-volume emergency department, continuing a tradition of service that reflects the spirit of this gathering. As the old saying goes, “We may be scattered far and wide, but when two or three shall meet, old tales be retold.” So break out your stories and share how your experience at NMH helped prepare you for military service.

NMH Boys' Soccer

7:30 to 9:30 p.m. EST

NMH History and Archives

If I want to make a gift before Feb. 26, will it count toward NMH Gives?

YES! You can make your gift online or mail a check with “NMH Gives” in the memo.

What if I already made a gift to NMH this year?

Thank you! Previous gifts do not count toward NMH Gives. Please consider making another gift of any amount to be counted toward NMH Gives.

Do I have to make a gift to the NMH Fund or an established class scholarship in order to be part of NMH Gives?

Yes. This is an NMH Fund giving day! Gifts to the NMH Fund go to work right away, supporting our students and the great work of our faculty, coaches, and advisors.

How can I make a gift?

  • Give online.

  • Mail a check to: Office of Advancement, NMH, One Lamplighter Way, Mount Hermon, MA 01354. Write “NMH Gives” in the memo.

  • Donate by phone at 1-866-NMH-GIVE (1-866-664-4483).

Will my NMH Gives contribution be tax deductible?

Absolutely. All gifts during NMH Gives are tax deductible, and all donors will receive a receipt for tax purposes.

How can I help spread the word?

Email Jocelyn Merrick, NMH Fund director, at jmerrick@nmhschool.org to learn how you can get involved and help make NMH Gives a success!

I still have questions. How can I get in touch?

Call the NMH Alumni Office at 413-498-3600.
